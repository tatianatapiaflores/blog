Gyoza

This is my sister in laws recipe, her gyoza are out of this world! This is a simple recipe but you might need a couple of extra hands. Folding each gyoza takes a while but it is so worth it.

Ingredients

  • 1 packet of gyoza wrappers
  • 1 1/2lb ground pork
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup of finely chopped green onions
  • 1 cup of finely chopped cabbage
  • 2 cloves of finely minced garlic
  • half an inch of finely minced ginger
  • a slash of sake
  • 1 tsp of sesame oil
  • 2 tsp soy sauce

Place the ground pork, sake, sesame oil, and soy sauce in a medium size bowl. Finely chop the garlic, green onion, cabbage, ginger, and mushrooms. Dump into the bowl with the ground pork.

Mix everything together, pull out the wrappers and a little bowl of water. Place 1/2 a tbsp of filling in the wrapper, enough so that the wrappers close. If the wrappers are over stuffed they will pop open when cooking. Wet the wrapper with the water and fold the square wrapper into a triangle.

Once all the wrappers are folded drop some oil into a medium sized pan on medium heat. Put the gyoza in one layer on the pan. Cook for 4 mins, than throw in 1/4 cup of water in the pan and cover for another 4 mins. Repeat until all gyoza are cooked, and crispy.
